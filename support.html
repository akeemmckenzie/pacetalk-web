Frequently Asked Questions

Why does the app need location access?
PaceTalk uses your device's GPS to calculate your running pace in real time. Without location access, the app cannot track your speed or distance. Background location access allows pace tracking and audio alerts to continue working when your screen is locked during a run. Location data is processed entirely on your device and is never sent to any server.
Does PaceTalk work offline?
Yes. PaceTalk works completely offline during a run. GPS tracking, pace calculation, and audio alerts all function without an internet connection. All your settings and run history are stored locally on your device. No internet connection is needed to use the app.
How do I set my pace zone?
On the home screen, use the MIN PACE and MAX PACE selectors to define your target pace range. MIN PACE is your fastest acceptable pace, and MAX PACE is your slowest. You can tap the pace value to type an exact time, or use the +/- buttons to adjust in 5-second increments. You can also enable or disable either limit using the toggles if you only want to set one boundary.
Why isn't my pace updating?
If your pace shows "--:--", this usually means one of the following: you are standing still or moving very slowly (below 0.5 m/s), your device's GPS signal is weak (try moving to an open area away from tall buildings), or location permissions have not been fully granted. Make sure both "While Using" and "Always" location permissions are enabled for PaceTalk in your device settings for the best experience.
How do I cancel or get a refund?
PaceTalk is currently free with no in-app purchases. If a paid feature is added in the future, any purchases would be handled through the App Store. To request a refund for an App Store purchase, visit reportaproblem.apple.com and follow Apple's refund process.
